Design a high-fidelity desktop pre-interview screen for PlacedOn, an AI-powered hiring platform.

  Goal:
  Create the final pre-interview “disclaimer + readiness” screen shown immediately before the live AI interview begins. This screen must build trust, explain the process clearly, disclose how AI and transcript
  data are used, show that all system checks have passed, and make the candidate feel calm and ready to start.

  This is not a legal wall of text.
  This is not a technical setup page.
  This is a premium, humane, trust-building start screen for a high-stakes interview experience.

  Core product context:
  - PlacedOn runs a 30-minute AI-led interview
  - the AI is disclosed clearly as AI
  - the interview is a conversation, not a test
  - the system generates a candidate profile from the interview transcript
  - employers see the profile, not the raw transcript
  - the candidate can review the profile later and flag anything inaccurate
  - accommodations must be acknowledged before start
  - text fallback must be available
  - whiteboard is available during the interview
  - the UI should feel calm, premium, and reassuring

  Visual direction:
  Use the design language from soft premium editorial SaaS and the provided inspiration references:
  - off-white or warm ivory canvas
  - large rounded outer shell
  - white and lightly tinted cards
  - subtle lilac, powder blue, and pale warm-beige atmospheric washes
  - deep graphite text
  - cobalt/periwinkle accent color
  - restrained success green for passed states only
  - very soft layered shadows
  - premium whitespace
  - elegant, refined typography
  - polished, mockup-worthy composition
  - humane and reassuring tone

  Avoid:
  - legalistic compliance-page feeling
  - boring setup wizard feeling
  - generic enterprise dashboard density
  - dark futuristic AI cyberpunk style
  - harsh gradients or neon glows
  - dense technical language
  - generic HR software look

  Canvas and layout:
  - desktop-first frame around 1440px wide
  - centered premium app shell with large radius and subtle shadow
  - two-column layout
  - left side around 60–65% width for content and process explanation
  - right side around 35–40% width for readiness panel, system checks, accommodations summary, consent, and CTA
  - soft background atmosphere behind the shell, very subtle and luxurious
  - layout should feel spacious and balanced, not crowded

  Header:
  - PlacedOn logo top-left
  - subtle context label near logo:
    Product Manager
    Round 1: Technical & Behavioral
  - optional top-right mini indicators:
    AI interview
    ~30 min
    Secure and private
  - keep the header elegant and minimal

  Main left content:
  Create a calm hero section with this emotional tone:
  supportive, clear, premium, non-threatening

  Primary heading:
  Before we begin

  Short intro copy:
  You’re about to start a 30-minute AI-led interview with PlacedOn.
  This is a conversation, not a test. There are no trick questions or perfect answers.
  We’re here to understand how you think, communicate, and approach problems.

  Below the intro, include a polished “What to expect” section with 4 clear steps in premium card or timeline format.

  Use these 4 steps:
  1. Warm-up
  A short opening to help you settle in.

  2. Situational questions
  You’ll talk through how you would approach realistic problems.

  3. Deeper follow-ups
  The interview may go deeper where your thinking shows the strongest signal.

  4. Profile review
  After the interview, you’ll be able to review the profile created from the conversation.

  Below that, include a clean, elegant “How your information is used” section.
  Use concise, reassuring language, not legal text.

  Include these points:
  - Your transcript is used to generate your interview profile
  - Employers can view your profile, not the raw transcript
  - You’ll be able to review your profile and flag anything that feels inaccurate
  - Your information is handled securely

  Right-side readiness panel:
  The right-side panel is the emotional anchor of the screen.
  It should feel like a premium readiness card, not a technical checklist.

  Panel headline:
  You’re ready to begin

  Optional supportive subtext:
  Everything looks good. You can still adjust your setup before starting.

  Inside this panel, include visually grouped readiness items with soft success indicators:
  - Camera connected
  - Microphone connected
  - Connection stable
  - Permissions granted
  - Whiteboard available
  - Text fallback available

  Make the checks feel elegant:
  - soft green or soft blue check icons
  - subtle icon badges
  - premium spacing
  - no harsh alert styling

  Accommodations section:
  Within the right panel, include a secondary block titled:
  Need anything adjusted?

  Include supportive copy:
  You can switch to text, change your setup, or update accommodations before starting.

  Show accommodation/status chips or rows such as:
  - Voice interview
  - Text fallback available
  - Extra response time: Off

  Include a button or text action:
  Update accommodations

  Consent section:
  Below readiness and accommodations, include three consent checkboxes with refined spacing and typography.

  Use this wording:
  - I understand this is an AI interview
  - I consent to transcript analysis for profile generation
  - I understand employers will see my profile, not the raw transcript

  CTA area:
  Primary button:
  Start interview

  Secondary button:
  Switch to text

  Tertiary text link:
  Resume later

  The CTA area should feel confident and premium.
  The primary button should be visually strong but not aggressive.
  The secondary action should remain clearly available for accessibility and comfort.

  Optional micro-details:
  - include a tiny helper note near the CTA such as:
    You can pause or switch formats later if needed.
  - include tiny icons for camera, microphone, shield/privacy, whiteboard, transcript, and accommodations
  - consider a very subtle illustrative or abstract visual accent on the left, but do not let it overpower the content
  - the whole page should feel designed, expensive, and calm

  Typography:
  - use premium modern typography similar to Manrope, Sora, Plus Jakarta Sans, or Inter
  - strong display hierarchy for the main title
  - soft but readable secondary copy
  - clear labels for sections
  - avoid overly corporate typography choices

  Component style:
  - large radii throughout
  - soft layered cards
  - subtle inner highlights
  - translucent or lightly tinted pills where useful
  - very clean iconography
  - premium button styling
  - excellent spacing rhythm
  - visual hierarchy must feel intentional and luxurious

  Color system suggestion:
  - background: warm off-white or ivory
  - shell: soft white with slight warmth
  - card tint 1: pale lilac-blue
  - card tint 2: soft warm beige
  - text primary: deep graphite
  - text secondary: muted grey-blue
  - primary accent: cobalt/periwinkle
  - success states: soft sage/green
  - avoid pure black and harsh saturated colors

  Important UX goals:
  - the user should understand the interview in under 10 seconds
  - the user should feel calmer after reading the screen
  - the user should trust the system more after reading the screen
  - the screen must communicate both clarity and polish
  - the screen should feel like a product moment, not a form

  Final output:
  - one polished desktop screen
  - fully resolved visual hierarchy
  - realistic copy
  - premium UI styling
  - ready for a production-quality product design direction

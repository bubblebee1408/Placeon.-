# PlacedOn Landing Page — Design Prompt

Use this prompt for Google Stitch, Emergent AI, or any AI design tool.

---

## Prompt

Design a minimal, modern landing page for **PlacedOn** — an AI-powered hiring platform that replaces resumes with AI-conducted interviews. The platform interviews candidates through conversation, builds verified skill profiles using AI inference, and matches them with companies based on real demonstrated ability — not self-reported claims.

---

### Color Palette

**Primary green palette — green represents growth, trust, positive outcomes.**

- Deep Green: `#0A6847` — primary backgrounds, hero sections, CTAs
- Bright Green: `#16A34A` — buttons, highlights, active states, progress indicators
- Mint / Light Green: `#DCFCE7` — subtle section backgrounds, cards, hover states
- Pale Green: `#F0FDF4` — page background, light sections
- Near-Black: `#1A1A1A` — headings, body text
- White: `#FFFFFF` — card backgrounds, contrast areas
- Warm Gray: `#F5F5F5` — dividers, muted backgrounds

Accent (sparingly): Soft Gold `#FACC15` for stars/ratings, or Slate `#64748B` for secondary text.

**No neon. No gradients. Clean, flat, trustworthy.**

---

### Typography

- **Font:** Plus Jakarta Sans (Google Fonts)
- **Headings:** Bold 700, large (36-48px hero, 28px sections)
- **Body:** Regular 400, 16-18px, generous line height (1.5-1.6)
- **Buttons:** SemiBold 600, 16px

---

### Visual Style

- **Minimal and clean** — lots of whitespace, no clutter
- **Rounded shapes** — border-radius 12-16px on cards, 24px on pills/tags, no sharp corners anywhere
- **Streamline "Plump Flat" icon style** — chunky, rounded, blob-like solid-fill icons. Friendly and approachable. The AI interviewer is represented as a friendly green blob character with a simple face.
- **Little motion graphics** — subtle animations only:
  - Gentle fade-in on scroll for each section
  - A soft pulsing or breathing animation on the AI avatar/blob
  - Skill tags that gently float or slide into view
  - A minimal animated flow diagram showing: Interview → AI Analyzes → Profile Generated → Matched with Companies
  - No aggressive parallax, no heavy video backgrounds, no flashy transitions
- **Illustrations over photos** — use flat illustrations of people interviewing, profiles being built, companies discovering candidates. No stock photos.

### Page Structure (Top to Bottom)

#### 1. Navigation Bar
- Logo ("PlacedOn" in bold Plus Jakarta Sans, green accent on the dot or "On")
- Nav links: How It Works, For Companies, For Candidates, Pricing
- Two CTAs: "Log In" (ghost button) and "Get Started" (solid green button)
- Sticky on scroll, white background with subtle bottom border

#### 2. Hero Section
- **Headline:** "Your skills speak louder than your resume."
- **Subheadline:** "PlacedOn interviews you with AI, builds a verified skill profile, and matches you with companies that actually fit — based on what you can do, not what you claim."
- **Two CTAs side by side:**
  - "Take Your First Interview" (solid green, primary)
  - "I'm Hiring" (outlined green, secondary)
- **Hero visual (right side or below on mobile):** A minimal illustration or animation showing:
  - A friendly green AI blob character having a conversation with a candidate
  - Skill tags floating out of the conversation (e.g., "Problem Solving," "System Design," "Collaboration")
  - These tags assembling into a profile card
- Keep the hero clean — no more than headline + subheadline + CTAs + one visual

#### 3. "How It Works" — Three Steps (Candidate Flow)
Horizontal three-column layout (stacks vertically on mobile). Each step has:
- A numbered circle (1, 2, 3) in green
- A plump flat illustration
- A short title + one sentence

**Step 1: "Pick a role and interview"**
"Choose a role you're targeting. Our AI interviewer has a 30-minute conversation with you — no trick questions, just real scenarios."
Illustration: candidate chatting with the green AI blob, a progress bar showing 60%

**Step 2: "AI builds your verified profile"**
"Our engine analyzes your responses and generates a skill profile with evidence. Every trait is backed by something you actually said."
Illustration: a profile card being assembled — skill tags snapping into place, a confidence meter filling up

**Step 3: "Companies find you"**
"Your profile is matched against open roles. When there's a fit, both sides get notified. No cold applications, no resume black holes."
Illustration: a candidate profile card and a job posting card connecting with a green line/bridge

Small subtle animation: the three steps connected by a dotted line that draws itself on scroll.

#### 4. "For Candidates" Section
Light green (`#F0FDF4`) background section.

**Headline:** "A profile that proves what you can do"

Feature grid (2x2 or 2x3), each with an icon + title + one line:

- **AI-Inferred Skills** — "Your skills are assessed through conversation, not self-reported. No exaggeration, no guesswork."
- **Verified Achievements** — "Upload certifications and achievements. The AI validates them in your next interview."
- **Growth Trajectory** — "Retake interviews as you grow. Companies see your improvement over time, not just a static snapshot."
- **You Control Visibility** — "Your public profile shows your top strengths. Full details are only shared when you apply or match."
- **Professional Summary** — "AI generates your professional summary from interview evidence. No more writing about yourself in third person."
- **Offers & Applications** — "Track every application and offer in one place. Know exactly where you stand."

#### 5. "For Companies" Section
White background section.

**Headline:** "Hire based on proof, not promises"

Feature grid:

- **Smart Job Postings** — "Write a job description naturally. Our AI structures the requirements and finds matching candidates automatically."
- **Evidence-Based Screening** — "Every candidate card shows AI-assessed skills. Hover any skill to see the exact interview moment that proved it."
- **Fit Score** — "Each candidate gets a contextual fit score for your specific role. Not a generic rating — a real match."
- **Two-Way Matching** — "We surface candidates who fit your role, even if they haven't applied. Both sides opt in before any contact."
- **Compare Candidates** — "View candidates side by side. Skills, evidence, growth trajectory — all in one view."
- **Manage Multiple Roles** — "Post and manage multiple openings. See applicants and recommended matches for each."

#### 6. "The AI Interview" — Trust Builder Section
Pale green background. This section addresses the obvious concern: "Can I trust an AI to assess people?"

**Headline:** "An interview designed to be fair"

Three short trust points with icons:

- **Bias-Checked** — "Every AI assessment runs through our ABLEIST bias framework — 8 metrics that catch subtle harms traditional tools miss entirely."
- **Evidence, Not Inference** — "The AI only reports what you actually demonstrated. No assumptions, no extrapolation."
- **You Validate** — "Candidates see their full AI-generated profile. If something feels off, retake the interview. The system stays honest."

Optional: a small animated diagram showing the Markov loop simplified: "You respond → AI updates understanding → Bias check → Next question" as a clean circular flow.

#### 7. "Who It's For" — Quick Audience Callout
Simple two-column layout:

**Left card:** "Fresh graduates" — "No work experience? No problem. PlacedOn assesses what you can do, not where you've been. Designed for India's 1.5M annual tech graduates."

**Right card:** "Growing startups" — "Stop reading 500 resumes. Let AI-verified profiles surface the candidates who actually match your needs. Starting at $299/month."

#### 8. CTA Section (Pre-Footer)
Dark green (`#0A6847`) background, white text.

**Headline:** "Ready to be placed based on what you can actually do?"
**Two CTAs:**
- "Start Your Interview" (white button with green text)
- "Post a Job" (outlined white button)

#### 9. Footer
Minimal. Dark background.
- PlacedOn logo
- Links: About, How It Works, For Candidates, For Companies, Pricing, Contact
- "Built in India" badge (optional)
- Social links (if applicable)
- Copyright

### Responsive Behavior

- Hero: side-by-side on desktop, stacked on mobile (visual below text)
- Feature grids: 2-3 columns on desktop, single column on mobile
- Three steps: horizontal on desktop, vertical timeline on mobile
- Navigation: hamburger menu on mobile with same CTAs visible
- All cards maintain 16px border-radius and generous padding on all breakpoints

---

### Motion Graphics Specification

Keep motion **subtle and purposeful** — no decorative animation for its own sake.

1. **Hero AI blob** — gentle idle breathing animation (slight scale oscillation, 3-4 second cycle). On page load, skill tags float up from the conversation one by one (staggered 200ms).
2. **Scroll reveals** — each section fades in + slides up 20px as it enters viewport. Duration 600ms, ease-out. Trigger once only.
3. **How It Works connector** — dotted line between the 3 steps draws itself left-to-right as user scrolls past. SVG stroke-dashoffset animation.
4. **Skill tags in Step 2** — snap into the profile card one at a time (staggered, slight bounce easing).
5. **Trust section loop** — the simplified Markov loop diagram rotates gently (one full cycle every 8 seconds), highlighting each step as it passes.
6. **CTA buttons** — subtle scale-up on hover (1.02x, 200ms). No color change animation.

**No parallax. No video backgrounds. No particle effects. No scroll-jacking.**

### Assets Needed

- PlacedOn logo (text-based, Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, green accent)
- Friendly green blob AI character (Streamline Plump Flat style, simple face — two dots for eyes, small curved mouth)
- 6-8 flat illustrations for feature sections (Plump Flat style, green palette)
- 3 illustrations for the "How It Works" steps
- Icon set in Plump Flat style for feature grids (~12 icons)
- Simple animated SVG for the Markov loop diagram

---

### What This Page Must Communicate in 10 Seconds

A first-time visitor scanning the hero should understand:
1. This replaces resumes with AI interviews
2. The AI builds your skill profile from the conversation
3. Companies find you based on verified skills, not claims
4. There are two clear entry points: take an interview, or post a job
